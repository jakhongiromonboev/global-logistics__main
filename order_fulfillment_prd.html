Product Requirements Document (PRD)

Learning Activity: Order Fulfillment Chain Breakdown – Analysis & Decision-Making Exercise

Objective / Purpose

To help students understand the real-world challenges of global order fulfillment by analyzing a realistic case, identifying root causes of delays or errors, and making strategic decisions based on logistics data.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the steps involved in global order fulfillment
  • Identify common real-world problems (e.g., customs delays, inventory misplacement)
  • Analyze logistics data to detect fulfillment weaknesses
  • Propose and justify improvements to reduce costs, delays, and customer dissatisfaction

Target Audience

Undergraduate or graduate students studying logistics, supply chain management, or international business

Key Features & Components

Component Description
Case Study Document A short real-world inspired scenario of a company facing global fulfillment challenges
Data Sheets Simplified logistics data sets (e.g., order volume, delivery time, shipping costs, customer satisfaction)
Decision Tasks Students must recommend changes to improve performance (e.g., reroute shipments, rebalance inventory)
Discussion Guide Questions to reflect on decisions and outcomes (can be used for class discussion or individual response)
Reflection Worksheet A form for students to summarize lessons learned and how they would handle similar situations in the future

Tools & Resources Needed

  • PDF or online version of the case study
  • Printable or digital data sheets
  • Worksheets or Google Forms for responses
  • Optional: spreadsheet templates for basic cost/delivery analysis

Evaluation Criteria

Metric Indicator
Engagement Student participation in analysis and discussion
Understanding Ability to explain fulfillment problems and suggest practical solutions
Application Quality and logic of decisions based on provided data
Reflection Insightfulness of student reflections on the activity outcomes

Estimated Duration

30–45 minutes (flexible for homework or classroom)

Success Indicators

  • Students show better understanding of how fulfillment works across global logistics systems
  • Improved ability to spot cause-effect relationships in supply chains
  • Active class discussions or thoughtful written responses
  • Positive feedback from students about activity clarity and usefulness
